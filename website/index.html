From Report to Repair

How RoadWatch PH Works

Every report starts with one caring citizen and ends with safer roads for the whole community. Here is the journey from first scan to final repair.

FOR CITIZENS

How Citizens Make a Difference

  1. 📱
    Scan the QR Code Spot RoadWatch PH QR codes in barangay halls, waiting sheds, or electric posts to quickly open the website.
  2. 📝
    Add Details, Snap a Photo, and Pin Tap Report Road Issue, complete the Google Form, upload a clear photo, and pinpoint the location on the map.
  3. 📬
    Submit and Track Submit instantly and check your email for a unique tracking number to monitor status updates in real time.

FOR AUTHORITIES

How Authorities Drive Solutions

  1. 🖥️
    Receive Reports Barangay, LGU, and DPWH teams receive incoming reports through the RoadWatch PH website in real time.
  2. 🚦
    Verify and Prioritize Officials validate submitted reports, assess severity, and prioritize urgent road concerns for faster action.
  3. 🔧
    Update Progress Repair teams update statuses like Pending, In Progress, and Repaired so citizens stay informed.
  4. 🤝
    Ensure Transparency Digital tracking keeps the process accountable and helps communities see that road concerns are being addressed.

Every update matters. Citizens report, authorities act, and communities stay informed together.

What You Can Report

Citizens can report different types of road and infrastructure problems to help authorities respond faster and improve road safety.

 ⌄

🛣️ Road Surface Problems

  • Potholes
  • Road cracks
  • Faded or missing road markings
  • Uneven or damaged road surfaces

🌧️ Drainage and Flooding Problems

  • Clogged roadside drainage
  • Missing or broken drainage cover
  • Flooded roads during heavy rain
  • Water pooling on the road

⚠️ Road Safety Issues

  • Malfunctioning traffic lights
  • Missing or damaged traffic signs
  • Lack of pedestrian crossings
  • Broken or missing guardrails

🏙️ Street Infrastructure Problems

  • Damaged sidewalk
  • Broken streetlights
  • Damaged road reflectors
  • Open or uncovered manholes

🚧 Road Obstruction Issues

  • Fallen trees blocking the road
  • Construction materials on the road
  • Illegal parking blocking lanes
  • Debris or large objects on the roadway

Every report helps agencies prioritize road repairs and improve safety for all road users.

About RoadWatch PH

RoadWatch PH is a national road reporting system designed to make it easier for citizens to report road-related issues in their communities. Through a mobile-friendly website and QR codes placed in public areas, residents can quickly submit reports by uploading photos, indicating the exact location, and providing a brief description of the problem.

Each report generates a unique tracking number sent to the user’s email, allowing citizens to monitor the status of their report—from pending to repaired—through real-time updates on the website. This tracking system ensures transparency and keeps citizens informed about the progress of road maintenance.

By connecting citizens with responsible authorities such as Barangay Officials, Local Government Units (LGUs), and the Department of Public Works and Highways, RoadWatch PH promotes faster response, improved coordination, and more efficient road management across the country.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Who can report road issues?

Any citizen who notices unsafe road conditions.

Do I need an account to report?

No account is required.

What road problems can be reported?

Potholes, flooding, cracked pavements, missing manhole covers, and other hazards.

How long does the repair take?

Repair time depends on the severity of the issue.

Can I report anonymously?

Yes, you can submit a report without revealing your identity.

How do I track my report?

Each report generates a unique tracking number that you can use to monitor its status online.

Will I be notified when the issue is fixed?

Yes, the system updates the status of your report, and you'll receive a notification once it is resolved.

What if the road problem is urgent?

For urgent issues that could cause accidents, report them immediately to the emergency contacts, and include clear details and photos.

Can businesses or organizations report issues?

Yes, both individuals and organizations can report road hazards.

Are all reports automatically sent to the authorities?

Yes, once submitted, reports are forwarded to the appropriate local government unit or agency responsible for road maintenance.

What if the road issue is already being repaired?

You can still report it; your report helps track ongoing repairs and ensures proper completion.

Can I report multiple issues at once?

Yes, you can submit separate reports for each road problem for better tracking.

Is there a cost to report a road issue?

No, reporting is completely free.

What information should I include in my report?

Include clear photos, nearby landmarks, accurate location, and a short description of the problem.

Who is responsible for fixing the road?

The local government unit or the agency in charge of road maintenance handles repairs.

Can I suggest a repair priority?

You can mention the urgency, but final decisions are made by the responsible authority.

What if the reported issue is outside my area?

Reports are forwarded to the proper local authority, even if it's outside your barangay or city.

Can I report road hazards caused by private construction?

Yes, you can report hazards from construction sites that affect public roads.

Is RoadWatch PH available nationwide?

Yes, the platform is designed for citizens across the Philippines.
